The New Computer

A 10-slide thesis on agentic systems and software authorship: models reason, agents act, and software becomes a medium far more people can use to build.

01

The Model as a Reasoning Substrate

The shift was not autocomplete. It was reasoning over context.

Large language models mattered because they could take context and produce useful thought. Once that was true, the next step was obvious: not just answering questions, but deciding what to do next.

02

The Agentic Loop

Put that reasoning inside a loop and you get an agent.

The same model that answers can inspect results, update its view, and choose the next move. Observe, decide, act, inspect, repeat. That changed the model from interface to operator.

03

Claude Code as the First Visceral Proof

Claude Code made the idea feel real.

It let people watch an AI read a codebase, plan, edit, run, fail, recover, and continue. Not because it invented every ingredient, but because it made outcome-seeking behavior visible.

04

The Environment Becomes the Surface Area

What matters is what the agent can touch.

A terminal, filesystem, browser, APIs, design tools, and scheduling turn model ability into real leverage. The boundary stops being what the model was built for and becomes what the environment exposes.

05

The Ralph Loop

Fresh context, persistent state, same goal until done.

Geoffrey Huntley described Ralph in the simplest possible way: a bash loop. Restart the agent, let the repo hold the state, and stop only when real checks pass. It belongs here because it showed that outcome-seeking behavior did not require a complex framework. A loop, external memory, and clear exit conditions were enough.

06

OpenClaw and the Move Beyond the Coding Box

OpenClaw asks what happens when the agent leaves the coding box.

Add background jobs, memory, scheduling, dynamic tools, and long-running workflows, and the agent stops looking like a coding assistant. It becomes a general-purpose computational actor.

07

The Broken Asymmetry of the Old Computer

Software was the one major creative medium most people could not touch.

You could write, record, design, and publish without becoming a specialist. Software usually demanded years of training and abstraction tolerance. Billions could use it. Very few could make it.

08

Software Becomes a Creative Medium

The payoff is abundance, not just niche personalization.

Agents make one-off tools viable. They also make breakout products, high-utility apps, and fast copycat waves easier to build for people whose edge is taste, judgment, and timing.

09

The New Literacy Is Specification

The new literacy is specification.

Natural language lowers the interface barrier, but rigor still matters. The skill is stating intent, setting constraints, judging outputs, and refining systems until they match the idea.

10

The Platform Shift Is Authorship at Scale

Automation is the first story. Authorship is the bigger one.

When software gets cheaper to make, the frontier is not only efficiency. It is expression: one-person tools, serious products, strange experiments, and companies built with leverage that once required teams.
